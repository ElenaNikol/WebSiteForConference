Ohrid


Nicknamed the ‘Jerusalem of the Balkans’, home to narrow, cobble stoned streets and medieval-esque architecture, vacationer’s paradise with glimmering beaches, spectacular views, and a wealth of unique Macedonian, Mediterranean and Turkish cuisine, Ohrid makes the perfect hosting city.


Settled on the Lake Ohrid, at three million years old, the lake stands as one of Europe’s oldest and deepest lakes, and gives Ohrid the prestige of being named as both a cultural and natural UNESCO World Heritage site.
